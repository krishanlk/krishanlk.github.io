Research Product Introduction

Diabetic foot ulcers (DFUs) are a common and serious complication of diabetes that can lead to infection, amputation and reduced quality of life. Early detection and monitoring of DFUs are essential for effective treatment and prevention of further complications. However, current methods of wound assessment and monitoring are often subjective, inaccurate and time-consuming. We have developed a novel system that uses machine learning to automatically assess and monitor DFUs using images captured by a smartphone camera. Our system can accurately detect the presence and location of DFUs, measure their size and depth, classify their severity and infection status, and track their healing progress over time. Our system also provides feedback and guidance to patients and clinicians on how to care for the wounds and prevent recurrence. ... Our system is based on state-of-the-art deep learning models that have been trained and validated on a large and diverse dataset of DFU images. Our system can handle various challenges such as low-quality images, different lighting conditions, occlusions, and skin tones. Our system has been evaluated on several metrics such as accuracy, precision, recall, mean average precision (mAP) and F1-score, and has achieved superior performance compared to existing methods. Our system is a breakthrough in the field of DFU detection and monitoring, as it offers a fast, reliable, convenient and cost-effective solution that can improve the outcomes and quality of life of patients with diabetes. Our system can also be easily integrated with existing telemedicine platforms and electronic health records to facilitate remote consultation and data sharing. Our system is a game-changer for the management of DFUs and the prevention of diabetic foot complications.

Methodology

Methodology

The research methodology for “Diabetic foot ulcer assessment and progress monitoring using machine learning” consists of five main steps: data collection, dataset preparation, model development, product development, and system deployment. We collected images and symptom data of diabetic foot ulcers from various sources, such as public datasets, we scrapping , and online platforms. We then prepared the datasets for different machine learning tasks, such as Wound detection, depth detection, area segmentation, and color detection. They also performed data preprocessing, such as duplicate removal, division into testing, training, and validation sets, and augmentation.


Next, We developed machine learning models using convolutional neural networks (CNNs) and other algorithms to extract and monitor observable characteristics of ulcers and related symptoms. We also designed and developed a healing score and risk assessment algorithm to predict the healing progress and pattern of the ulcer. Furthermore, We developed a web-based product that integrates the machine learning models and provides a user-friendly interface for diabetic patients to assess their ulcers. The product also includes a conversation approach to capture symptom. Finally, We deployed the system on a cloud platform and evaluated its performance using various metrics, such as accuracy, precision, recall, F1-score.

Findings


  • DFU Detection:
  • System achieves 97% accuracy in ulcer detection.
  • Outperforms other detection models such as MobilenetV2, DFU VIRNet, DFUNet, and DFU QUTNet.
  • DFU Depth Detection:
  • Novel approach identifies ulcer depth with 96% accuracy.
  • Eliminates the need for expensive thermal equipment used in previous studies.
  • DFU Area Segmentation:
  • EfficientNet model achieves high accuracy (99%), IoU score (96%), and F1 score (98%).
  • Provides accurate segmentation of the ulcer area and wound measurement.
  • Healing Score and Risk Assessment:
  • Weightage-based algorithm predicts healing stage by incorporating symptoms and DFU factors.
  • - Provides a comprehensive assessment beyond image analysis.
  • Web/Mobile-based Telemedicine System:
  • Develops a user-friendly web-based tool for DFU assessment.
  • Integrates multiple factors, advanced machine learning models, and a patient-centered design.
  • Enhances DFU management for patients and clinicians.
