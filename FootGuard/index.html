Vision


To revolutionize the management and monitoring of diabetic foot ulcers (DFUs) through the power of artificial intelligence, improving the lives of individuals living with diabetes worldwide.

Mission


Develop AI tool for accessible, accurate, user-friendly assessment and progress monitoring of DFUs, providing timely information, personalized care, and insights, reducing amputations.



Goals


  • Develop an AI-based tool that accurately assesses the presence, depth, and characteristics of diabetic foot ulcers using image analysis and machine learning algorithms.
  • Create a user-friendly interface that allows patients and medical professionals to easily access and utilize the tool for efficient monitoring and management of DFUs.
  • Integrate conversational-based symptom tracking to capture valuable patient-reported data and enhance the holistic assessment of DFUs.
  • Continuously improve the performance and accuracy of the tool through ongoing research, development, and refinement of machine learning models and algorithms.
  • Establish a comprehensive database to store and analyze historical DFU data, enabling further insights, research, and development in the field of DFU management.
  • Collaborate with healthcare providers, researchers, and stakeholders to ensure the widespread adoption and implementation of our AI-based tool in clinical settings.
  • Contribute to the reduction of DFU-related amputations by providing timely interventions, personalized care plans, and actionable insights to healthcare professionals.
  • Continually innovate and expand the capabilities of our tool to address the evolving needs of diabetic patients and healthcare systems, with a focus on improving health outcomes and enhancing the quality of life for individuals living with diabetes.

Research Product Introduction

Diabetic foot ulcers (DFUs) are a common and serious complication of diabetes that can lead to infection, amputation and reduced quality of life. Early detection and monitoring of DFUs are essential for effective treatment and prevention of further complications. However, current methods of wound assessment and monitoring are often subjective, inaccurate and time-consuming. We have developed a novel system that uses machine learning to automatically assess and monitor DFUs using images captured by a smartphone camera. Our system can accurately detect the presence and location of DFUs, measure their size and depth, classify their severity and infection status, and track their healing progress over time. Our system also provides feedback and guidance to patients and clinicians on how to care for the wounds and prevent recurrence. ... Our system is based on state-of-the-art deep learning models that have been trained and validated on a large and diverse dataset of DFU images. Our system can handle various challenges such as low-quality images, different lighting conditions, occlusions, and skin tones. Our system has been evaluated on several metrics such as accuracy, precision, recall, mean average precision (mAP) and F1-score, and has achieved superior performance compared to existing methods. Our system is a breakthrough in the field of DFU detection and monitoring, as it offers a fast, reliable, convenient and cost-effective solution that can improve the outcomes and quality of life of patients with diabetes. Our system can also be easily integrated with existing telemedicine platforms and electronic health records to facilitate remote consultation and data sharing. Our system is a game-changer for the management of DFUs and the prevention of diabetic foot complications.

Methodology

The research methodology for “Diabetic foot ulcer assessment and progress monitoring using machine learning” consists of five main steps: data collection, dataset preparation, model development, product development, and system deployment. We collected images and symptom data of diabetic foot ulcers from various sources, such as public datasets, hospitals, and online platforms. We then prepared the datasets for different machine learning tasks, such as ulcer detection, depth detection, area segmentation, and color segmentation. They also performed data preprocessing, such as duplicate removal, division into testing, training, and validation sets, and augmentation.

Next, they developed machine learning models using convolutional neural networks (CNNs) and other algorithms to extract and monitor observable characteristics of ulcers and related symptoms. We also designed and developed a healing score and risk assessment algorithm to predict the healing progress and pattern of the ulcer. Furthermore, We developed a web-based product that integrates the machine learning models and provides a user-friendly interface for diabetic patients to assess their ulcers. The product also includes a chat assistant that can capture symptom data using natural conversation manner. Finally, We deployed the system on a cloud platform and evaluated its performance using various metrics, such as accuracy, precision, recall, F1-score, and Dice coefficient.
